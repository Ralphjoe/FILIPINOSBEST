Lumpiang Shanghai

Ingredients

  • 500g ground pork
  • 1 small carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 small onion, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lumpia wrappers (spring roll wrappers)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Mix ground pork, carrot, onion, garlic, egg, soy sauce, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  2. Place a tablespoon of the mixture on a lumpia wrapper, fold, and roll tightly.
  3. Seal the edge with water or beaten egg.
  4. Heat oil and deep fry the rolls until golden brown.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve with sweet chili sauce.

Dynamite Lumpia

Ingredients

  • 12 large green chili peppers (siling haba)
  • 200g ground pork or beef (optional)
  • 12 strips of cheese (cheddar or quickmelt)
  • Lumpia wrappers
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Slit the chilies lengthwise and remove the seeds.
  2. Stuff each chili with a strip of cheese (and optional ground meat).
  3. Wrap each stuffed chili with a lumpia wrapper, leaving the stem exposed.
  4. Seal the wrapper with water or beaten egg.
  5. Deep fry in hot oil until golden brown.
  6. Drain on paper towels and serve with a dipping sauce of choice.

Kwek-Kwek

Ingredients

  • 12 quail eggs, hard-boiled and peeled
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • Orange food coloring (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, water, salt, pepper, and food coloring to make a smooth batter.
  2. Coat each quail egg lightly with cornstarch.
  3. Dip the eggs into the batter, coating them completely.
  4. Deep fry the eggs until golden and crispy.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve with spiced vinegar or sweet chili sauce.

Tokwa't Baboy

Ingredients

  • 250g pork belly, boiled and cubed
  • 200g firm tofu, cubed and fried
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small chili (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil pork belly until tender, then cut into cubes.
  2. Deep fry tofu until golden and crispy.
  3. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, onion, chili, salt, and pepper to make the dipping sauce.
  4. Combine the pork and tofu in a serving dish, then pour the sauce over or serve it on the side.
